Gear Trains: Technical Overview and Profile Application

Definition

A gear train is a mechanical system formed by mounting two or more gears on a frame to transmit power and motion between shafts. The gears within a train are in mesh, with the teeth of one gear engaging the teeth of an adjacent gear.

Primary Technical Purposes

  • Modification of Angular Velocity: Gear trains are used to achieve a specific velocity ratio (VR) between the input (driver) and output (driven) shafts. VR = ωout / ωin = Nin / Nout, where ω is angular velocity and N is the number of teeth.
  • Torque Amplification/Reduction: Concurrently with velocity changes, torque (T) is altered: Tout = VR ⋅ Tin ⋅ η (where η is efficiency). Reducing speed increases torque, and vice-versa.
  • Change in Direction of Rotation: External gears in mesh reverse the direction of rotation. An idler gear can be used to maintain the same direction between driver and driven gears or to bridge a gap.
  • Power Transmission over Distance: Connecting shafts that may be offset or at an angle.

Application of Involute and Cycloidal Gear Sets

Gear trains can be constructed using gears with various tooth profiles, most commonly involute or, less frequently in modern applications, cycloidal profiles.

In your specific context, you are utilizing:

These sets, when assembled into gear trains, will leverage the characteristics of the involute profile, such as:

  • Constant pressure angle and smooth transmission of motion.
  • Tolerance to minor variations in center distance without affecting the velocity ratio.
  • Suitability for high-speed and high-power applications due to favorable stress distribution and ease of manufacture with high precision.

These sets, when used to form separate gear trains, will exhibit properties of the cycloidal profile, including:

  • Potentially lower friction and wear due to more rolling contact, especially in pinions with a small number of teeth.
  • Absence of interference issues, allowing for pinions with fewer teeth than typically feasible with involute profiles without modification.
  • However, they require precise center distances for correct conjugate action and have a variable pressure angle.

Compound Gear Set Configuration in the Kit

For demonstrating compound gear trains, the kit typically utilizes the following Involute gears:

  • Two (2) 150mm diameter Involute spur gears.
  • Two (2) 110mm diameter Involute spur gears.

A common compound gear train setup involves:

  • An input shaft (driver) with one of the gears (e.g., a 110mm gear).
  • An intermediate (compound) shaft where one 150mm Involute gear and one 110mm Involute gear are coupled together to rotate on the same axis. The 150mm gear on this shaft meshes with the input gear, and the 110mm gear on this same shaft acts as the driver for the next stage.
  • An output shaft (driven) with the remaining 150mm Involute gear, which meshes with the 110mm gear from the compound shaft.

This configuration allows for a significant change in the overall gear ratio compared to a simple gear train using the same gears individually.

Important Note on Usage

As specified, your involute gear sets and cycloidal gear sets are employed independently. This means an involute gear train will consist solely of involute gears, and a cycloidal gear train will consist solely of cycloidal gears. Mixing profiles within a single direct meshing train is not standard practice and would lead to incompatible motion.

Gear Trains in the Kit

Our demonstrator kit allows for the assembly of both Simple and Compound Gear Trains using the provided Involute spur gears. The flexible mounting system on the baseboard facilitates various configurations.

Key Components for Gear Trains:
  • A set of Involute spur gears of varying sizes (e.g., 150mm and 110mm diameter gears).
  • Multiple shafts that can be mounted on the baseboard.
  • Bearings or bushings for smooth rotation of shafts.
Assembly Insights:
  • For a Simple Gear Train, typically two gears are mounted on separate shafts, meshing directly.
  • For a Compound Gear Train, one 150mm Involute gear and one 110mm Involute gear are coupled on the same intermediate shaft.
