Assembly and Disassembly Instructions

Please read these instructions carefully before assembling or disassembling the kit. Use the diagrams to identify the necessary parts.

Assembling a Simple Gear Train

Follow these steps to assemble a simple gear train using the provided components. This setup typically involves two or three gears.

Ensure the demonstration base is clean and placed on a stable surface. Identify suitable mounting points for two or more gear shafts that can be aligned for a simple gear train.

Select a 150mm gear (either Involute or Cycloidal, as per experiment requirements) and mount it onto one of the prepared shafts on the base. This will typically be your main driven gear or an idler.

Select a 110mm gear (matching the tooth profile of the 150mm gear if they are to mesh directly, or as a driver/driven gear in the train). Mount it on an adjacent shaft, ensuring it meshes correctly with the 150mm gear or is positioned appropriately for the train configuration.

Attach the provided handle to the input shaft (axis) of the gear train. This will typically be the shaft of the gear you intend to rotate manually (e.g., the 110mm gear if it's the driver).

After all components are fixed in a secured way, gently rotate the handle. Observe the motion of all gears in the train. Check for smooth operation, proper meshing, and any unexpected noises or binding.

Assembling a Compound Gear Train

Follow these steps to assemble a compound gear train. This setup involves multiple gears, with at least one shaft carrying more than one gear.

Ensure the demonstration base is ready, providing stable mounting points for multiple shafts required for a compound gear train configuration.

Mount a 150mm Involute gear onto a primary shaft on the base. This could be an initial driver or a part of the first stage of the compound train.

Select an intermediate shaft. Mount a 110mm Involute gear and another 150mm Involute gear onto this same shaft, ensuring they are fixed together (e.g., keyed or press-fitted) to rotate as a single unit. The 150mm gear on this shaft might mesh with the previously mounted gear, and the 110mm gear will drive the next stage.

Mount another 110mm Involute gear on a separate shaft, positioned to mesh with the 110mm gear on the compound shaft. This will be the final driven gear of the compound train.

Attach the handle to the axis of the initial driver gear of the compound train.

After all components are fixed in a secured way, gently rotate the handle. Observe the motion of all gears in the compound train. Check for smooth operation and proper meshing throughout the train.

Changing Center Distance between Two Spur Gears

Follow these steps to understand how to adjust and observe the effects of changing the center distance between two meshing spur gears. This is crucial for proper gear operation, affecting clearance and backlash.

Ensure the demonstration base is set up with provisions for adjusting shaft positions, specifically for altering the center distance between two gears.

Mount a 150mm gear (select either Involute or Cycloidal profile for this experiment) onto one of the adjustable shafts on the base.

Mount a 110mm gear (of the same tooth profile as the 150mm gear) on another adjustable shaft. Initially, position this shaft using a specific mounting hole or slot that provides a known, slightly larger center distance (e.g., a hole designated for a +3mm adjusted center distance from the ideal). Ensure the gears mesh.

Attach the handle to the axis of one of the gears, typically the smaller (110mm) gear, which will act as the driver for this observation.

After all components are fixed securely, gently rotate the handle. Observe the meshing quality, noting any excessive backlash or noise due to the increased center distance. Then, if your kit allows, adjust the center distance to the ideal (or a smaller value) and observe the difference in operation. Repeat with different center distance settings to demonstrate its effect.

Assembling the Pulley and Belt System

Follow these steps to assemble the flat pulley and belt system, including the load cell for tension measurement.

Ensure the demonstration base is clear and identify the mounting locations for the pulley shafts and the load cell.

Take the 100mm outer diameter pulley and mount it onto its designated shaft on the base. Ensure it is securely fastened and can rotate freely.

Carefully loop the flat belt around the groove of the 100mm pulley.

Take the 80mm outer diameter pulley and mount it onto its designated shaft on the base. This shaft might be adjustable for belt tensioning.

Loop the other end of the flat belt around the 80mm pulley. Adjust the position of the pulleys (or use a tensioning mechanism if provided) to achieve appropriate initial belt tension. The belt should be snug but not overly tight.

If your kit includes a load cell for measuring belt tension, mount it securely on the base according to the kit's design. It might be integrated with a tensioning pulley or a mechanism that interacts with the belt.

Attach the handle to the shaft of the driving pulley (e.g., the 100mm pulley or as specified).

After all components are fixed in a secure way, then rotate the handle and measure belt tension.

Assembling the CAM-Follower Mechanism

Follow these steps to assemble the CAM and Follower mechanism, including motor and electronics setup for motion graphing.

Ensure the main demonstration base is prepared, identifying mounting points for the motor, CAM shaft supports, and the follower module.

Securely mount the motor onto the designated position on the base (basement). Ensure it is aligned correctly for connecting to the CAM shaft.

Select the desired CAM profile (e.g., Eccentric, Heart, Pear, or Snail Drop CAM). Securely attach this CAM to the axial shaft. Ensure it is fixed and will not slip during rotation.

Mount the axial shaft (with the CAM attached) onto the base supports/bearings. Connect one end of the shaft to the motor output, possibly using a coupling if provided.

If the follower mechanism requires a spring to maintain contact between the follower and the CAM, insert the spring into the follower module as per the design.

Mount the assembled follower module onto the base, ensuring that the follower tip is in proper contact with the CAM profile. Align it correctly according to the mechanism's design.

Install sensors (e.g., rotary encoder for CAM angle, linear potentiometer for follower displacement), Arduino board, and motor driver control unit onto the base or designated electronics panel. Make necessary wiring connections as per the circuit diagram (ensure power is off).

After all components are fixed and electronics are connected (double-check connections), power up the system. Rotate the CAM (either manually via a handle if designed, or using the motor). Record the follower's displacement at various CAM angles. Plot this data to obtain the follower's motion graph.
