Project Experiments & Objectives

Explore the core objectives of the Mechanism Demonstrator Kit through practical experiments.

01. Gear and Pinion Sets (Cycloidal & Involute Teeth)

Understanding two sets of Spur gears: one with Cycloidal teeth and another with Involute teeth. Key focus on demonstrating 'Clearance' and the ability to control 'Center Distance'.

Procedure:
  1. Identify the Involute and Cycloidal gear sets.
  2. Mount the Involute gears. Observe and measure initial 'Clearance'.
  3. Adjust 'Center Distance' for Involute gears and observe changes in meshing and 'Clearance'.
  4. Replace with Cycloidal gears. Observe 'Clearance' and sensitivity to 'Center Distance' changes.

Expected Outcome: Understand sensitivity differences to center distance for both profiles.

02. Simple Gear Train (Involute Spur Gears)

Focuses on constructing and understanding a 'Simple Gear Train' using 'Involute spur gears'. Key focus on gear ratio and direction of rotation.

Procedure:
  1. Select two different sized Involute spur gears.
  2. Mount them to mesh correctly, forming a driver and driven gear.
  3. Count teeth (N_driver, N_driven) and calculate theoretical Gear Ratio (N_driven / N_driver).
  4. Rotate driver one full turn, count driven gear turns for experimental ratio. Observe rotation direction.

Expected Outcome: Calculate and verify gear ratio and predict output motion.

03. Compound Gear Train (Involute Spur Gears)

Constructing and analyzing a 'Compound Gear Train' to achieve larger gear ratios and understanding the train value.

Procedure:
  1. Select at least four gears to form a compound train (e.g., Gear A driving B, Gear C on same shaft as B, driving D).
  2. Assemble the train, ensuring gears on compound shafts are fixed together.
  3. Count teeth for all gears (N_A, N_B, N_C, N_D).
  4. Calculate theoretical Train Value: TV = (N_B/N_A) * (N_D/N_C).
  5. Rotate input (Gear A) one turn, count output (Gear D) turns. Compare with TV.

Expected Outcome: Understand how larger speed reductions are achieved with compound trains.

04. Flat Pulleys and Belt System

Demonstrating a 'Flat pulley' and belt system, with a focus on measuring and demonstrating 'belt tensions' and observing slip.

Procedure:
  1. Assemble the flat pulleys and belt system.
  2. Apply an initial low tension to the belt. Measure this tension if possible.
  3. Rotate the driver pulley and observe for slip at the driven pulley, especially under slight load.
  4. Incrementally increase belt tension, measure, and observe slip changes.

Expected Outcome: Understand the relationship between belt tension and power transmission capability.

05. CAM Mechanism and Follower Motion

Exploring the 'CAM' mechanism and 'graphing the motion of the follower', understanding how CAM profile dictates motion, and analyzing the displacement diagram.

Procedure:
  1. Assemble the CAM and follower mechanism.
  2. Set CAM to 0 degrees. Measure and record follower displacement.
  3. Rotate CAM by fixed increments (e.g., 15 degrees).
  4. Record follower displacement at each increment for a full 360-degree CAM rotation.
  5. Plot Displacement (Y-axis) vs. CAM Angle (X-axis).
  6. Compare Your Result and Sensors Output.

Expected Outcome: Generate and interpret a CAM follower displacement diagram.

06. Demonstration Frame/Board

Understanding the setup and use of the frame or appropriate board designed to demonstrate all the above mechanisms. This includes familiarization, mounting/interchanging mechanisms, and general handling.

Procedure:
  1. Inspect the demonstration frame/board and identify all mounting points and adjustment features.
  2. Practice attaching one mechanism module (e.g., the belt drive system) to the frame according to instructions.
  3. Practice detaching the module and attaching a different one (e.g., the CAM mechanism).
  4. Review overall stability and safety considerations when using the frame with different mechanisms.

Expected Outcome: Confidently set up and switch between various mechanism demonstrations on the board.
